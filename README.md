# HR Analytics Dashboard
## Short Description

- An interactive HR analytics dashboard that visualizes employee attrition, demographics, salary trends, and workforce distribution to uncover actionable insights for HR decision-making.

 ## 🧰 Tools & Skills Used
- Tool: Power BI
- Data Cleaning & Transformation
- Data Visualization
- DAX / Calculated Measures
- Analytical Thinking
## 📂 Data Source
- Dataset inspired by an HR analytics tutorial from YouTube  
## 🚀 Features & Highlights
- 📌 Overview metrics (Total Employees, Attrition, Attrition Rate, Avg Age, Salary, Tenure)
## 📊 Attrition analysis by:
- Age group
- Education field
- Salary slab
- Years at company
- Job role
- 👥 Gender-based attrition comparison
- 🧩 Department-level filtering (HR, R&D, Sales)
- 📈 Trend analysis for employee exits
## 👀 Key Visuals
- Donut chart: Attrition by education
- Bar charts: Attrition by age, job role, and salary slab
- Line/area chart: Attrition by years at company
- KPI cards for high-level metrics
- Matrix table for job role breakdown
## 🎯 Goal of the Dashboard

- To help HR teams identify patterns in employee attrition and support data-driven strategies for improving retention and workforce planning.

## 🔍 Key Insights
- Highest attrition observed in the 26–35 age group
- Employees with lower salary slabs (≤5K) show significantly higher attrition
- Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives have the highest turnover
- Majority of attrition occurs within the first few years of employment
- Slightly higher attrition among male employees compared to female
